Overview


The human species has evolved to become the most dominant species on the planet, and populates almost 100% of the globe as well as being the top of the food chain in all ecosystems. However, we are not the strongest or the fastest, so how is it that we have become such a dominant force on the planet earth? even more so than the dinosaurs of old and the sharks that rule the sea after our relatively short existance

We humans have one aspect that sets us apart from almost all animals on the planet, and that is our brain. The human brain is able to solve complex problems and adapt to almost every situation it encounters, however it does have its drawbacks. A large brain like ours requires vast amounts of energy to function, grow and learn.

This page will be underlining some of the theories proposed as to why we, as a species have been able to develop our great levels of intelligence and begin to conquer the world since our species first began to evolve around 2 million years ago.

The Use of Tools


While not completely unique to humans, tools have been a core part of our evolution. Our use of early tools such as the hand axes, spears, cutting tools, and arrows have propelled us in the evolutionary race. Due to the evolution of the opposing thumb in apes, humans and other apes are able to have a much greater dexterity and control wnen it comes to precise movements and actions with our hands. This ability and the relatively high intelligence possesed by early humans allowed us to create tools from the environment around us, form flints, wood and bone. these tools allowed early humans to have a huge amount of success when it came to hunting and accesing food, as we were able to make up for any lacking in offensive power with piercing weapons and blunt force.

This constructive weaponry would allow us as humans to provide energy for our rapidly developing and growing brains leading to ever greater advancements in technology, such as the bow and arrow for example. it was tools like these that allowed humans to hunt and gather in safety, being able to take down dnagerous prey at a range and deending against attackers. this overall would lead to a massive increace in the population of the early huamn, boosting them to the top of almost every food chain.

Running Man


We humans are no means the quickest, strongest or most agile of creatures, in fact we are probably out done by most animals that we would encounter in at least one of these aspects and usually more. So why is it that we were able, despite our weak physical appearence, able to hunt and kill animals many times our size?

The reason behind this is that we are designed purely for endurance. Being bipedal meant that we were able to travel large distances without stopping or slowing down. Due to our upright nature, we are able to supply constant oxygen to our vital organs and muscles without it being impeded when we are running. Quadrupeds however, when running, their are forced aginst their ribcage, reducing oxygen capcity and preventing them from being able to cool down. Taking this factor into account, our ancestors were able to apply a relentless advance towards the animal, preventing it from stopping to pant and unable to cool down, eventually leading to its capture.

It was with this technique that we were able to combat the ice age beasts such as the bison, wooly mammoth, and the wooly rhino. With this ability, we were able to provide the energy required for our intelligence to develop.
